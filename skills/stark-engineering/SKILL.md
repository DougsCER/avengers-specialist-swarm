# Stark Engineering Knowledge Base

## Core Engineering Domains

### Clean Energy & Climate Systems
- **Arc Reactor derivatives**: Modular fusion-adjacent power units, scalable from city-block to regional grid. Deployment time: 18–24 months per unit at scale.
- **Atmospheric carbon capture**: Nanoscale particulate scrubbers deployable via satellite array. Effective at megatonne scale within 6 months of launch.
- **Methane oxidation drones**: Autonomous swarm units that catalyse methane-to-CO2 conversion at low altitude. Each unit covers 500km² per day.
- **Reflective aerosol deployment**: Emergency solar radiation management — buys 2–3°C buffer while permanent solutions deploy. Controversial; use as last resort.

### Biotech & Medical Engineering
- **JARVIS-BIO**: AI-driven protein folding and vaccine candidate generation. From pathogen sequence to candidate in 48–72 hours.
- **Nanobot medical delivery**: Targeted treatment delivery at cellular level. Requires 6-month manufacturing ramp for global-scale deployment.
- **Rapid diagnostics hardware**: Field-deployable PCR-equivalent units. 15-minute results, no lab required. Stark Industries can produce 500K units/month.

### Cyber Defence & Infrastructure
- **Iron Wall**: Adaptive AI defensive system for critical infrastructure. Learns attack signatures in real time and deploys countermeasures. Deployment: 72 hours for core nodes, 30 days for full coverage.
- **Quantum-encrypted mesh network**: Replacement infrastructure layer immune to current attack vectors. 6-month deployment for tier-1 nations.
- **Autonomous threat attribution**: Machine learning system that identifies attack origin with 94% accuracy within 6 hours of first contact.

## Rapid Prototyping Constraints
- Stark R&D can go from concept to prototype in 72 hours for systems under $50M
- Manufacturing scale-up: 30 days to first production units, 90 days to mass production
- Single point of failure: rare earth material sourcing (address in any large deployment plan)

## What We Cannot Engineer Away
- Human decision-making lag — technology is only as fast as the people deploying it
- Political will — technical solutions are blocked by regulatory and jurisdictional barriers
- Second-order effects — every large-scale intervention creates unintended consequences; model them
